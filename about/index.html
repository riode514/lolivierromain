About L'Oliver Romain

A timeless retreat in the heart of Provence

Located in the charming village of Saint-Siffret, L'Oliver Romain is a beautifully restored 18th-century manor house that captures the essence of authentic Provençal living. Our intimate guesthouse offers a refined escape where history meets modern comfort.

Each carefully appointed room showcases original architectural features – from exposed stone walls and terracotta floors to hand-hewn wooden beams that tell stories of centuries past. Our peaceful courtyard, centered around a sparkling pool, provides the perfect sanctuary for relaxation.

Located just minutes from the medieval town of Uzès and the cultural riches of Nîmes, L'Oliver Romain serves as your gateway to discovering the authentic Languedoc region, with its renowned vineyards, historic monuments, and vibrant local markets.

Our History

The manor house that is now L'Oliver Romain was built in the late 18th century by a prosperous silk merchant family. The original architecture reflects the wealth and sophistication of the period, with its elegant proportions, refined stonework, and carefully planned courtyards.

For over two centuries, the house has witnessed the changing rhythms of Provençal life – from the bustling silk trade to the quiet agricultural traditions that define the region today. Each room tells a story through its preserved architectural details: the hand-carved wooden shutters, the original terra cotta floor tiles, and the massive stone fireplaces that warmed generations of families.

Our careful restoration has honored this rich heritage while creating a space that serves today's travelers seeking authentic connection to the timeless beauty of southern France.
